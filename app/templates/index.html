Welcome to Algorithm Visualizer!

Algorithm Visualizer is an interactive online tool designed to bring the beauty of algorithms and data structures to life. Originally developed as a learning aid for the "Algorithms and Data Structures" university course, this tool offers both students and enthusiasts a unique and visually engaging way to understand and appreciate these fundamental aspects of computer science.

Here, you will find a selection of sorting, searching, and graph algorithms, each presented with a step-by-step visual demonstration using modern web technologies. By providing real-time visualization of each algorithm in action, Algorithm Visualizer turns abstract concepts into tangible and understandable processes. Enjoy exploring the world of algorithms and data structures!

Explore Key Algorithms in Computer Science

Sorting Algorithms

Sorting algorithms are essential tools in computer science. They allow us to organize complex data structures and are at the heart of many common tasks, such as data analysis and problem-solving. Whether you're looking to understand the basics of bubble sort, or delve into the complexities of quick or merge sort, our interactive visualizations offer a hands-on way to learn and understand these important algorithms.

Searching Algorithms

Searching is one of the most common tasks in computer science. It involves finding one or more items in a dataset, such as an array or a database. The efficiency of a searching algorithm can greatly influence the performance of a system. Explore linear, binary and recursive search algorithms through our interactive visualizations, and understand the principles that drive the search process.

Traversal Algorithms

Traversal algorithms play a pivotal role in data structure handling. They allow us to navigate through data structures, notably trees and graphs, and access or search for elements within. This process is integral to many operations such as searching, deletion, and insertion of nodes in data structures. Common traversal algorithms for trees includes In-Order, Pre-Order, and Post-Order traversals. Our platform provides interactive visualizations to help understand these essential algorithms in a hands-on manner.
